KMART ANNOUNCES PROMOTIONS, RESTRUCTURING IN MERCHANDISE PRESENTATION DEPARTMENT

TROY, Mich., Aug. 13, 1996 -- Kmart Corporation today announced additional promotions in its merchandise presentation department to more effectively integrate the retailer's in-store communication and promotional programs with the recently announced restructuring of the retailer's merchandising organization.

The following promotions of Kmart executives are effective immediately: Chad R. Harrison, 35, will become Divisional Vice President - Softlines Merchandise Presentation; Kenneth M. Kramer, 43, will become Divisional Vice President - Hardlines and Consumables Merchandise Presentation; and, Ronald J. Hudgens, 41, will become Divisional Vice President, Visual Merchandising. The three Kmart veterans will continue to report to Cecil B. Kearse, Vice President, Merchandise Presentation and Communication.

"Whether buying clothes for their children, fashions for their home, or every day necessities, customers expect value-priced merchandise that is easy to find and attractively displayed every time they shop at Kmart," said Kearse. "By refining our merchandise presentation support organization, we can develop better national promotions, create more dynamic in-store displays and improve the overall shopping ambiance within our stores nationwide. With these improvements, our stores will be more enjoyable for our customers to shop, and in turn, generate more sales for Kmart."

Harrison began his career with Kmart in 1983 in Green Bay, Wisc. and has held apparel positions of increasing responsibility at Kmart's headquarters and in various regions of the country including stores in Wisconsin, Oklahoma and Texas. Kramer joined Kmart in 1973 in Sioux City, Iowa and held various store and field management positions before becoming divisional sales manager at Kmart headquarters in 1995. Hudgens joined Kmart Apparel Corporation in 1977 in Greenville, S. C. and held positions in apparel in the Southern Region until 1990 when he moved to Kmart headquarters. For the past 2 years, Hudgens has been responsible for visual merchandising improvements for Kmart stores nationwide.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,147 Kmart and 168 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through a joint venture, to Mexico.
