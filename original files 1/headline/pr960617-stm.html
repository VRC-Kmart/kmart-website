KMART CLOSES NEW $3.7 BILLION CREDIT FACILITIES AND
$1 BILLION OFFERING OF TRUST CONVERTIBLE PREFERRED SECURITIES

Proceeds Used To Retire Previous Credit Facilities

TROY, Mich., June 17, 1996 -- Kmart Corporation (NYSE: KM) today announced that it has completed more than $4.7 billion in new financings that provide the Company with additional liquidity and enhance its financial strength and flexibility.

Kmart today simultaneously closed its new, three-year $2.5 billion Revolving Credit Facility and $1.2 billion Term Loan Facility and its $1 billion public offering of 7.75% trust convertible preferred securities. As planned, the Company has used a portion of the proceeds from these financings to repay its previous credit facilities in full and to retire or reacquire certain of its real estate debt. The balance of the proceeds will be used to provide the Company with additional flexibility as it executes its strategic business plan.

"The completion of the credit agreements and securities offering represent a tremendous step forward in the turnaround of Kmart," said Floyd Hall, Chairman, President and Chief Executive Officer of Kmart Corporation. "These financings have given us the time and financial flexibility we need to properly execute our new merchandising, marketing and operational initiatives and maintain strong vendor support. The strong response to these financings, both of which were oversubscribed, demonstrates the financial community's confidence that Kmart has the right team and strategic plan to restore the Company to profitability and growth."

"We were very pleased by the outstanding work done by Chase Manhattan Corporation in syndicating the credit facilities and by the co-lead underwriters of the equity offering, Morgan Stanley & Co., Inc. and Merrill Lynch & Co.," Hall continued. "Now that these financings have been successfully completed, the members of Kmart's senior management can return our full attention to running our business and implementing our strategic plan."

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,159 Kmart and 167 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through joint ventures, to Mexico and Singapore.
