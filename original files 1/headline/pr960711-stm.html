KMART CORPORATION AND DAKA INTERNATIONAL TO CREATE
JOINT VENTURE TO OPERATE KMART STORE RESTAURANTS

TROY, Mich. -- Kmart Corporation (NYSE: KM) today announced it has signed a letter of intent to form a joint venture company with DAKA International, Inc. (NASDAQ: DKAI), a Danvers, Mass.-based food service and restaurant company. The joint-venture company will operate restaurants in 1,850 Kmart and Super Kmart stores. The parties intend to sign a definitive agreement and complete the transaction, which is subject to compliance with the Hart-Scott-Rodino Act filing requirements and other conditions, effective as of Sept. 1, 1996. The proposed transaction is consistent with Kmart's strategy to outsource labor intensive, specialized service departments.

As outlined in the letter of intent, DAKA will have a 51 percent ownership interest in the joint venture and Kmart will have a 49 percent ownership interest. The new company will operate all restaurants in existing Kmart and Super Kmart stores as well as all new stores opened during the initial 15-year term of the agreement. The Kmart restaurant operations generated approximately $350 million in annual sales volume in 1995.

"This joint venture is another action within our turnaround strategy to focus on our core discount business and build partnerships that improve the performance of all of our operations," said Floyd Hall, chairman, president and CEO of Kmart. "By joining with a high quality partner such as DAKA, we will bring a wealth of food service management expertise to what has been an under performing area of our business. As a result, we expect to offer our customers a better place to eat and relax during their visits to Kmart and, at the same time, significantly improve the financial performance of our restaurant operations."

William H. Baumhauer, chairman and CEO of DAKA said, "Our relationship with Kmart will benefit both companies going forward. The creation of the new venture provides DAKA an incredible opportunity to add value to the Kmart shopper with our retail-oriented food service expertise, at the same time we build national brand equity for our well-received signature concepts. Our experience, proven concepts, appealing menus, prices and effective cost controls will benefit both partners, and ultimately, the Kmart shopper."

William T. Freeman, 36, will serve as president of the joint venture, and will report to a five-member Board of Directors to be comprised of three representatives of DAKA and two members from Kmart. Freeman previously served as president of the Specialty Products Group within DAKA International, where he spearheaded the development of French Quarter Coffee and Good Natured Cafe, and directed the Great Bagel and Coffee Company acquisition.

Approximately 12,000 Kmart food operations associates will become employees of the new joint venture company upon completion of the transaction. During the next three years, DAKA and Kmart will invest a total of $100 million in capital, commensurate with the ownership ratio, to upgrade the food service facilities and supporting information technology of all of the Kmart-based restaurants. The new company will operate the restaurants in Kmart and Super Kmart stores, including approximately 550 Little Caesars. DAKA will grant to Kmart a warrant to purchase up to 1.5 million shares of DAKA International common stock, at an exercise price of $35 per share, if the fair market value of the joint venture company is at least at $300 million as of the fifth anniversary of completion of the transaction.

DAKA International is a diversified, growth-oriented food service and restaurant company which provides food service management through Daka Restaurants in the United States with over 700 food locations; owns or franchises 194 Fuddruckers restaurants in the United States, Canada, Australia and the Middle East; and owns or franchises 20 Champps casual dining restaurants throughout the United States. The Specialty Concepts Division, serving both the Daka Restaurant and Fuddruckers concepts, as well as over 350 unaffiliated sites, is a leading innovator in developing retail, cinema and other non-traditional dining locations.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,147 Kmart and 168 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through a joint venture, to Mexico.
