KMART LAUNCHES NEW PRIVATE LABEL CREDIT CARD NATIONWIDE
Features Rich Rewards Program For Customers

TROY, Mich. -- Kmart Corporation today announced that, after a highly successful test, its new private label credit card has been rolled out nationwide effective Aug. 26, 1996. The credit card features up to a 6 percent rebate of annual eligible card purchases, one of the richest rewards programs in the country.

Customers will receive "Kmart Rewards" good for merchandise only in any Kmart or Super Kmart store. Rewards will be issued each November in time for holiday shopping. In addition to the "Kmart Rewards", advance notice of sales and special discount offers will be offered to credit card holders. Customers can apply for an instant credit decision at any Kmart location nationwide. The card will have no annual fee and feature low monthly payments.

"Our customers recognize the added convenience of shopping with the Kmart credit card and that the 'Rewards' program provides them with even greater savings for their shopping dollar," said Floyd Hall, Kmart chairman, president and CEO. "Customer response to the Kmart credit card test has been excellent. In fact, during the test Kmart card holders shopped our stores more often and their average transaction was higher than that of non-card holders."

The Kmart credit card, offered through Beneficial National Bank USA, a unit of Beneficial Corporation, and various incentive programs were tested in 11 markets throughout the United States earlier this year. The rewards program proved to be the most popular with customers.

The partnership joins Kmart's store and customer network with Beneficial National Bank USA's financial product and expertise. Beneficial National Bank USA makes the credit decision and carries the financial risk for the portfolio outstandings.

Finn M. W. Caspersen, chairman and chief executive officer of Beneficial Corporation, noted that, "Based upon the results of the test program, Beneficial Bank USA and Kmart look forward to a long and very rewarding partnership. BNB USA has the systems to offer on-the-spot credit decision for Kmart's eligible customers."

Beneficial Corporation is a $15 billion, New York Stock Exchange-listed financial services holding company. Beneficial National Bank USA has more than $3.0 billion in total receivables and a card holder base of more than 5 million customers. Subsidiaries of Beneficial Corporation provide financial services through their various consumer-finance, banking and insurance operation located throughout the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Ireland and Germany.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,147 Kmart and 168 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through a joint venture, to Mexico.
