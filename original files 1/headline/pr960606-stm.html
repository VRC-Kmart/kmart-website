KMART PRICES AND INCREASES SIZE OF OFFERING OF TRUST CONVERTIBLE PREFERRED SECURITIES

Strong Response Prompts Increase In Size By $120 Million; Kmart Executives End "Road Show" Early Due To Oversubscription

TROY, Mich., June 6, 1996--Kmart Corporation (NYSE: KM) today announced that it has priced a public offering of 7.75% trust convertible preferred securities at $50 per share. Kmart also announced that, due to strong interest in the financial community, the underwriters increased the size of the offering from 15 million to 17.4 million securities, plus an underwriters overallotment option of 2.6 million securities. If the underwriters exercise their overallotment option, the value of the offering will be $1 billion.

Kmart intends to use the net proceeds of the offering in conjunction with a $3.7 billion credit facility to enhance its financial strength and flexibility. The securities offering and bank credit facility are each expected to close on June 17, 1996. The underwriters for these securities were Morgan Stanley & Co., Inc., Merrill Lynch & Co., CS First Boston, Lehman Brothers and Salomon Brothers Inc.

"We are very pleased by the tremendous response to our securities offering in the financial community," said Floyd Hall, Chairman, President and Chief Executive Officer of Kmart. "The oversubscription of the offering is a strong vote of confidence from Wall Street that we have the right team and strategic plan to achieve a successful turnaround of Kmart. Combined with our new three-year credit facility arranged by Chase Manhattan, this offering will give us the time and flexibility we need to properly execute our new merchandising, marketing and operational initiatives and maintain strong vendor support."

"I was particularly gratified by the terrific work done by co-lead underwriters Morgan Stanley and Merrill Lynch, which enabled us to increase the size of the offering under the most favorable terms contemplated," Hall continued. "Due to the strong initial response to the offering, the members of Kmart's senior management team were able to end our 'road show' almost a week early and return our full attention to running our business and continuing the turnaround of Kmart."

Distributions on the trust convertible preferred securities will be payable quarterly at the annual rate of 7.75% of the liquidation amount of $50 per security. Each trust convertible preferred security will be convertible at the option of the holder into shares of Kmart common stock at the rate of 3.33 shares of Kmart common stock for each trust convertible preferred security (equivalent to a conversion price of $15 per share of Kmart common stock), subject to adjustment in certain circumstances and other conditions. The trust convertible preferred securities will be payable on June 15, 2016. More information about this offering is contained in a prospectus filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,159 Kmart and 167 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through joint ventures, to Mexico and Singapore.
