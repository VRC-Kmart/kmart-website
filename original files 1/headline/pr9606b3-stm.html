KMART ANNOUNCES STOCKHOLDER VOTING RESULTS

TROY, Mich. -- Kmart Corporation today announced it has been advised by CT Corporation System, the independent inspectors of election, that the company's slate of directors and the three company proposals presented at its May 21, 1996 Annual Meeting were approved, and that five stockholder proposals presented at the meeting were defeated, based on CT's preliminary vote tabulation (copy attached).

The announcement of the results of the stockholder vote was not made at the Annual Meeting as is customary because an independent audit of the vote was conducted by CT this year, due to a proxy solicitation conducted by two unions. The preliminary results were provided to Kmart by CT today, and are subject to customary review at the request of the parties to the contest. The final results are expected to be certified shortly.

In its report, CT advised the company that a proxy service organization had indicated a desire to modify its proxy vote, but that such notice was not received until several days following the Annual Meeting and closing of the polls, and any change would be inappropriate.

The following directors were elected for the following terms: James O. Welch, Jr. for a term expiring in 1997, and James B. Adamson, Stephen F. Bollenbach, Floyd Hall, Robert D. Kennedy and William P. Weber for terms expiring in 1999.

The company proposals which were approved by Kmart stockholders were: amendments to the company's Directors Stock Plan and Stock Option Plan, as well as ratification of Price Waterhouse LLP as the company's independent accountants for fiscal 1996.

The stockholder proposals which were defeated concerned: amendments to the company's Stock Option Plan making certain awards contingent upon stock price increases; adoption of cumulative voting; elimination of the classified board; elimination of the Directors Retirement Plan; and conduct of a Board feasibility study regarding a sale or merger of the company.

"We are pleased with the results, and look forward to a productive year for Kmart," said Floyd Hall, Kmart chairman, president and CEO.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,149 Kmart and 167 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through joint ventures, to Mexico and Singapore.
