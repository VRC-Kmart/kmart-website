KMART CORPORATION AND DAKA INTERNATIONAL TERMINATE JOINT-VENTURE TALKS

TROY, Mich. -- Kmart Corporation (NYSE: KM) and DAKA International, Inc. (NASDAQ: DKAI), a Danvers, Mass.-based food service and restaurant company, today announced jointly they had terminated discussions to establish a joint venture company to operate restaurants in Kmart and Super Kmart stores. The parties had signed a letter of intent on July 11, 1996.

"While it is unfortunate that the joint venture could not be completed, the business analysis of the past several months will help us explore other options to improve the financial performance and efficiency of our restaurant operations," said Floyd Hall, chairman, president and CEO of Kmart.

William H. Baumhauer, chairman and CEO of DAKA International, Inc. stated, "While we are disappointed that we will not be going forward with the joint venture, we believe our shareholders will be well served by our intention to focus on our core business. We plan to concentrate on improving profitability and realizing the opportunities for growth we have identified within these operations."

DAKA International, Inc. owns Fuddruckers, Inc. which operates or franchises over 200 Fuddruckers restaurants in the United States, Canada, Australia and the Middle East; Daka, Inc., a leading provider of foodservice management in the United States with approximately $300 million in managed volume; and Champps Entertainment, Inc., a theme-based, media oriented restaurant concept in select locations coast to coast.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,144 Kmart and 167 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through a joint venture, to Mexico.
