KMART CORPORATION ANNOUNCES EXECUTIVE MOVES
Names E. Anthony Vaal, Vice President Far East Operations
Larry J. Foster, Vice President, Field Human Resources

TROY, Mich. -- Kmart Corporation today announced the appointment of E. Anthony (Tony) Vaal, 52, to the newly created post of Vice President, Far East Operations and Larry J. Foster, 44, Vice President, Field Human Resources, both effective July 22, 1996.

Vaal, a 29-year Kmart veteran, will directly report to William D. Underwood, Senior Vice President, International Sourcing, Quality and Product Development and will be responsible for the management of merchandise purchasing and sourcing in the Far East based in Hong Kong. Foster, a 22-year Kmart veteran, will directly report to Warren Cooper, Executive Vice President, Human Resources and Administration and will be responsible for the management and implementation of all human resource policies in the company's 2,147 stores nationwide. With Foster's appointment, Michael T. Macik will become Vice President, Human Resources for Kmart International Headquarters and Distribution Centers.

In announcing Vaal's new post, Warren Flick, Executive Vice President and President, Merchandising for Kmart said: "Tony Vaal is a seasoned executive, with hardlines, softlines, operations and vendor development experience who also understands the merchandising and logistics strategies we are implementing throughout Kmart. By elevating the responsibility for direct oversight of our Far East operation to that of a corporate officer, we can further strengthen our strategic vendor alliances, improve efficiency of our operations and better ensure end-to-end accountability for the quality of the goods we import."

In announcing Foster's promotion, Warren Cooper, said: "Larry Foster is a respected and talented executive who understands the strategic importance of having a committed, energetic, customer-oriented work force in all of our stores nationwide. With extensive store-level as well as field human resource experience, Larry will play an instrumental role as we improve all of our human resources operations, especially training and development, performance measures and related incentive programs."

Background on Vaal and Foster
Vaal, began his Kmart career in 1967 at the S.S. Kresge store in Speedway, Ind. With 13 years of store operations experience, Vaal was first promoted to a buying post at corporate headquarters in 1980. Since that time, he has held merchandising positions of increasing responsibility, including posts as Divisional Merchandise Manager for Menswear and Divisional Vice President, Hardlines Merchandising responsible for Toys, Sporting Goods, Seasonal, Greeting Cards, Giftwrap and Party Goods. For the past two years, Vaal has served as Divisional Vice President, Business Development responsible for the international administration of Kmart's import operations at the Company's Troy headquarters and various aspects of vendor development.

With the appointment of Vaal to his new post, Robert E. Larson, Director Far East Operations has announced his retirement effective July 31, 1996 after 36 years of service with Kmart, the past three based in Hong Kong.

Foster began his Kmart career in 1974 as a management trainee in Kalamazoo, Mich. With 16 years of store operations experience, Foster was first promoted to Regional Director for the East/Central regions and moved to Kmart headquarters in 1990. In 1992, Foster became Senior Director, Field Human Resources. In 1994, Foster became Divisional Vice President, Store Operations - Hardlines and in 1995 was promoted to the post of Operations Vice President.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,147 traditional discount and Super Kmart Centers, as well as 168 Builders Square Stores. Serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, Guam, the U.S. Virgin Islands, Kmart's international operations extend to Canada, and, through a joint venture, to Mexico.
