KMART REORGANIZES DISTRIBUTION OPERATIONS
Closing Alsip, Ill. and Sunnyvale, Tex. Distribution Centers

TROY, Mich., June 13, 1996 -- Consistent with Kmart Corporation's commitment to improve its overall distribution productivity and merchandise flow efficiency, the Company today announced it will close two under utilized distribution centers. The facilities, one in Alsip, Ill. that employs 191 associates and the other in Sunnyvale, Tex. that employs 132 associates, are expected to close on or about Aug. 15, 1996.

"While we regret any time that jobs are lost, our company must be fully productive to be competitive. By closing these facilities we will streamline our supply chain logistics, which is a critical element of the strategic turnaround plan that is now in place at Kmart under Floyd Hall's leadership," said Joseph J. Collins, senior vice president, logistics and vendor administration. "Within the logistics organization, we are committed to reducing cycle times, lowering costs, enhancing merchandise flow, upgrading our systems and improving our gross margin return on investment."

The Alsip and Sunnyvale facilities each have been operating with minimal shifts, while the Company's other distribution centers regularly run two or three work shifts each day. As a result of the reorganization, the work previously performed by the Alsip and Sunnyvale facilities will be absorbed by softlines distribution centers in Forest Park, Ga., Groveport, Ohio, and North Bergen, N.J.. Shifts are expected to be expanded at these three softlines facilities. The Company also operates a softlines distribution center in Carson, Calif. and 13 hardlines distribution centers nationwide, none of which will be affected by the announced reorganization plan.

Associates at the Alsip and Sunnyvale facilities will be eligible to receive severance packages and other assistance.


KMART CLOSES TWO DISTRIBUTION CENTERS
Closing Alsip, Ill. and Sunnyvale, Tex. Distribution Centers

The Alsip, Ill., softlines facility, located south of Chicago, opened in 1977 and serves 305 stores in 13 states. The Sunnyvale, Tex. softlines facility, located outside of Dallas, opened in 1988 and serves 282 stores in 16 states and Mexico. Both facilities are leased and will be marketed to other prospective tenants.

Kmart Corporation employs 300,000 and serves America with 2,149 Kmart and 168 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada, and through joint ventures, to Mexico and Singapore.
