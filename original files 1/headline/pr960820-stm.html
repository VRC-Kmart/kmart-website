KMART CORPORATION APPOINTS WILLIAM N. ANDERSON
TO GMM HARDLINES POST

TROY, Mich., Aug. 20, 1996 -- Kmart Corporation today announced that William N. Anderson, 49, will join the company as Senior Vice President and General Merchandise Manager, Hardlines, effective Sept. 3, 1996. In his new post, Anderson will report directly to Warren Flick, Executive Vice President and President, Merchandising, U.S. Kmart Stores and will be responsible for the categories of: sporting goods, hardware, paint and home improvement, toys, stationery, trim-a-home, horticulture and outdoor furniture, and do-it-yourself automotive supplies.

"Bill Anderson has a keen understanding of mass retailing, category management and hardlines merchandising that will serve Kmart well as we enhance the assortments, selection and value we offer our customers," said Flick.

Anderson joins Kmart from Oshman's Sporting Goods where he was President and Chief Operating Officer for the past two years. Previously, he held senior merchandising and retail operations posts at Ames Department Stores, Domain, Inc., Remco, Inc., Abraham & Straus, and Korvettes, Inc. Anderson is a graduate of Columbia University and the Harvard Business School.

In a related move, Andy Giancamilli, 46, will continue as Senior Vice President and General Merchandise Manager, Consumables but will have added responsiblity for the categories of home electronics, music and video, housewares and home decor, pet supplies, party goods, gift wrap and cards, as well as consumables. Giancamilli will report directly to Flick, as does Stephen Ross, Senior Vice President and General Merchandise Manager, Softlines who is responsibile for all apparel and soft-home categories at Kmart.

Responsibility for the traditional "drug store" categories at Kmart, including health and beauty care, photo processing, photography and over-the-counter drugs and pharmacies, will move to Jerry Kuske, who is promoted to the new specialty post of Vice President and General Merchandise Manager, Health and Beauty Care / Pharmacy. Kuske will report directly to Ronald J. Floto, Executive Vice President and President, Super Kmart Centers, with a "dotted line" reporting relationship to Flick. Kuske joined Kmart in 1995, having begun his retailing career with PayLess Drug Stores, followed by posts at Perry Drug Stores and Staples.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,147 Kmart and 168 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada, and through a joint venture, to Mexico.
