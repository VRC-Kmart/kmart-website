KMART CORPORATION ENTERS INTO A STRATEGIC ALLIANCE
WITH CARDINAL HEALTH, INC. AS EXCLUSIVE PHARMACY SUPPLIER

TROY, Mich. and DUBLIN, Ohio -- Kmart Corporation and Cardinal Health, Inc. today announced that they have signed a letter of intent to enter into a pharmacy strategic alliance with Cardinal Health, Inc., serving as the retailer's exclusive supplier to its pharmacies nationwide.

The target date for implementation of the new arrangement is August 1, 1996, subject to the signing of a definitive agreement and regulatory approvals. Kmart will continue to operate its 1,670 pharmacies which employ approximately 6,900 associates and fill 61 million prescriptions annually, and Cardinal will assume responsibility for direct replenishment of the Kmart pharmacies nationwide.

"By selling our pharmacy inventory to a leading-edge company like Cardinal, we will maximize the sales and profitability of our pharmacy operations and improve our inventory replenishment capabilities," said Floyd Hall, Kmart chairman, president and CEO. "At the same time, this partnership with a recognized leader in the areas of managed care, marketing and product procurement, will allow us to invest in the growth and services available to our pharmacy customers."

"We have enjoyed an excellent working relationship with Kmart for the past several years during which Kmart has grown its pharmacy business substantially," said Robert D. Walter, chairman and chief executive officer of Cardinal Health, Inc. "Our relationship will be significantly expanded with this new agreement in a number of ways, including repackaging services and sophisticated information links between Cardinal and Kmart. We have identified a number of unique opportunities to leverage our resources beyond the provision of traditional logistical services which will create value for both companies in innovative ways."

Cardinal Health, Inc., based in Dublin, Ohio, is one of the country's leading health care service providers. The company provides an array of value-added pharmaceutical distribution services to a broad base of customers nationwide. Through its wholly-owned subsidiary, Pyxis Corporation, Cardinal also develops and manufactures unique point-of-use systems which automate the distribution management and control of medications and supplies in hospitals and alternate care facilities. In addition, the company is a franchisor of apothecary-style retail pharmacies through its wholly-owned subsidiary, Medicine Shoppe, International, Inc.

Kmart Corporation serves America with 2,149 Kmart and 167 Builders Square retail outlets. In addition to serving all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the U.S. virgin Islands and Guam, Kmart operations extend to Canada and, through joint ventures, to Mexico and Singapore.
